Hooking Eyeballs on Social

How write blog posts people will read

By Yehor | 10/7/25

Lede

Don't Google "Hooked Eyeball Injury" but instead ask yourself: why did bloggers (and now, social media posters) speak of hooking eyeballs? Obviously, before a blog reader or social media user decides whether to read a post, they first need to notice it and decide they care about it. The image, title and lede of the post must grab the interest of those who will read the rest - and if it is sufficiently engaging, like, comment, and boost traffic.

Blog History

Before Insta and Snap, before Facebook, were the Blogs: "web logs" that served as open diaries or journals. Blogs got really big after WordPress, which let people create and share commentable blogs for free. This is why over 25% of all websites are made with Wordpress now.

Nothing is Free

Time is money (if you weren't reading this blog you could be making money doing something else) and attention can be bought (and sold). Advertisers might, for example, put an ad for fishing lures here, because the hero image of the post and the concept of eyeballs and hooks made them think it would attract fishermen.
